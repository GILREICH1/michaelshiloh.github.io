About me

I have a passion for building things, taking things apart, and using the bits that I find to make new things. I love experimenting with new ideas, new devices, and new techniques. I like using parts in ways for which they were not intended. I like setting up situations where people have experiences that they may not have otherwise.

My research interests include how people behave around robots, unusual mechanisms, and surprising uses of technology.

My practice includes participatory art, machine art, experimental sound art, and conceptual furniture.

I received my BSc in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from the University of California at Berkeley. My professional experience includes computer hardware, firmware, and designing embedded systems. I'm a strong advocate for Open Source, encouraging collaboration and sharing of information.

Working with art started when I was young, tinkering with electronics. I wasn't interested in practical devices. I wanted to make devices that were surprising or caused a sense of joy or wonder. I learned to weld and work with machines in the machine shop of a Kibbutz and further developed my metalworking skills and my artistic style in the San Francisco Bay Area machine art scene, particularly with Survival Research Labs, with whom I've worked since 1994. My conceptual furniture series started in 1991.

I am currently an Associate Professor of Practice of Interactive Media at New York University Abu Dhabi. I teach electronics, programming, mechatronics, robotics, design, critique of technology, and experimental interfaces. Prior to NYU Abu Dhabi I was an Associate Professor of Critical Studies at the California College of the Arts. You can find my full CV here.
