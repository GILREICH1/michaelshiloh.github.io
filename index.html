About me

I have a passion for building things, and learning how to build things. I like taking stuff apart and using the bits that I find. I love experimenting with new ideas, new devices, and new techniques. I like teaching students how to make stuff.

My research interests include creating environments in which people who consider themselves not good at making try making, participatory installations, discovering and developing interesting mechanisms, and taking things apart.

My practice includes machine art and conceptual furniture.

I received my BSc in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from the University of California at Berkeley. My professional experience includes computer hardware, firmware, and designing embedded systems. I'm a strong advocate for Open Source, encouraging collaboration and sharing of information.

I am currently an Associate Professor of Practice of Interactive Media at New York University Abu Dhabi. My subjects include electronics, programming, mechatronics, robotics, design, critique of technology and experimental interfaces. Prior to NYU Abu Dhabi I was an Associate Professor of Critical Studies at the California College of the Arts. You can find my full CV here.
