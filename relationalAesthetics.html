Relational Aesthetics and Participatory Art

In this category of my projects, the work displayed must be understood not as objects but as sites of interaction. Relational Aesthetics is a phrase coined by Nicolas Bourriaud in his book Relational Aesthetics (1998). Bourriaud asserts that the interpersonal relationships that develop in the context of these projects have formal qualities, and it is these formal qualities that can give such a work of art its value.

Ten discarded office photocopiers/printers/fax machines are presented for disassembly. Two of the units are on drop clothes with the necessary hand and powertools nearby. Aprons hang on the wall. Two large workbenches with hot melt glue guns are available for construction activities.

Pedestals showcase items that have been made with a previously disassembled printer. Some of these are kinetic, utilizing motors extracted from the printer, while others are static. Some pedestals are empty, inviting participants to showcase their own projects. Some pedestals display motors or motorized platforms, inviting participants to utilize the motion in some way in their own creations
